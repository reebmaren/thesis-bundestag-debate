Overview

Digital humanities researchers can effectively navigate and analyze large data sets, uncover patterns, and reveal hidden relationships using visualization techniques such as network diagrams, timelines, and data-driven visualizations. These visual representations serve as powerful tools for hypothesis generation, exploration, and communication of research results.

Visual heuristics in the digital humanities not only help explore data, but also enable researchers to communicate their findings to a broader audience in a more engaging and accessible way. The creation of the visualization based on the project data is explained on the following pages.

Preview

The creation of visualizations in the project and their exploratory potential will be presented. In the present work, the graphical representations were used on the one hand to make the intermediate results easier to present and to make them more understandable. On the other hand, the visualizations themselves were also used as a basis for the evaluation of the results.
