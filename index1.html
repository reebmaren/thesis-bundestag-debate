Term Frequencies

To determine the frequency of the selected keywords, the raw data of the speeches were first lemmatized. Punctuation, numbers, and stop words were removed. After that, the speeches of each year were combined into a single data file. In each of these files, the strings were split into a list. Each element was counted. The output was a nested dictionary called wordcnt. The outer key is the year and the value is another dictionary. The inner keys of these dictionaries are terms and the value is term frequency. A keyword can be searched in this dictionary. The example "integration" shows this. The output is a list of frequencies in each year of the 73 years of corpus. As in this example, a scatterplot was first generated to make the output more readable and easier to interpret. The distribution of the scatterplot already allows an interpretation. It shows that the frequency of the term "integration" starts to increase at the beginning of the 1990s, after having appeared continuously before. However, this did not mean that the most meaningful representation had been found.
